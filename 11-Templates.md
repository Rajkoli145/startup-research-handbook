> *"A framework you have to reconstruct from memory every time you need it isn't a framework. It's a nice idea you had once."*

## Why This Chapter Exists

Every chapter in this handbook has introduced at least one structured template — the Problem Statement (Chapter 1), the Dork Session Log (Chapter 2), the Reddit Research Log (Chapter 3), and so on through Chapter 10's Master Validation Scorecard. This chapter collects all of them in one place so you have a single reference to copy from during active research, instead of flipping back through ten chapters to find the right table.

Nothing here is new theory. This is your working binder.

## Common Mistakes When Using Templates

### Mistake 1: Treating a Template as a One-Time Form
Most of these are living logs, not one-off worksheets — the Dork Session Log, Reddit Research Log, and Competitor Tracker are all meant to be added to continuously, not filled out once.

### Mistake 2: Skipping Fields That Feel Obvious
The "why" fields (Structural Weakness, Five Whys, root cause) are the ones people skip first under time pressure — and they're the fields that produce the actual insight. A partially filled template is worth less than it looks.

### Mistake 3: Not Centralizing Templates in One Place
Keep all of these in a single connected workspace (a spreadsheet workbook or a linked notes system) so a finding in one log can be cross-referenced against another — this is how you catch patterns like "this complaint showed up in both Reddit and GitHub Issues."
